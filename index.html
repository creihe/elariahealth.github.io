About Us

Founder's Story

I didn't set out to start a company. I set out to figure out why mental health treatment keeps failing people.

During residency, I kept seeing the same pattern: patients would improve with treatment, then plateau or regress. We'd switch medications, try new therapies, but we were always reacting—never predicting, never adapting in real-time.

The problem wasn't the treatments. It was our model. We were treating mental health like a fixed problem when it's actually a continuous, adaptive process shaped by every interaction and experience.

I started building a framework to model longitudinal mental health development—how people actually change over time. And then I realized: this isn't just a clinical tool. This is a fundamentally different way to build AI.

What if you could create systems that learn continuously from experience, the way humans do? Not retrained on massive datasets, but adapting in real-time based on interaction?

That question became Elaria. I filed a provisional patent, incorporated as an LLC, and started building. I'm finishing residency and entering practice in July 2026, but I'm not doing this halfway. Elaria is what I'm committed to for the long term.

Right now, it's just me and an idea with serious potential. I'm looking for the founding engineer who wants to turn that potential into reality.

Our Values

Innovation

We don't optimize existing solutions—we build new ones.Elaria exists because the old models weren't working. We're not here to make incremental improvements to established approaches; we're here to rethink foundational assumptions. If something can be done better by starting from scratch, we start from scratch.

Impact Over Optics

We measure success by outcomes, not press releases.Elaria's technology needs to work in the real world—improving mental health, enabling better robotics, creating genuinely adaptive systems. We're not here to raise funding rounds or win awards. We're here to build technology that materially improves lives.

Integrity

We build technology we trust.Our architecture came from mental health research—a domain where mistakes have real human consequences. That standard doesn't change as we expand into other fields. We don't cut corners on safety, ethics, or transparency. If we wouldn't use it ourselves or recommend it to people we care about, we don't ship it.

Excellence

Good enough isn't good enough when you're building something new.We're creating a novel approach to AI—that means the bar is higher, not lower. Our work will be scrutinized, tested, and compared to established methods. We hold ourselves to rigorous standards because we have to earn credibility, and because the problems we're solving deserve our best work.

Resourcefulness

We solve problems with what we have, not what we wish we had.Elaria is bootstrapped and pre-revenue. That means we get creative, we prioritize ruthlessly, and we don't wait for perfect conditions to ship. Constraints breed creativity—we treat limited resources as a feature, not a bug.
